About Me

Three years ago, I said, “I will never learn how to program". I was an aspiring artist, but little did I know that my paintbrush would guide me to a world of digitization. It started in middle school when I participated in a technology summer camp. I learned how to use blocks of code to program lego robots and create new apps and video games online. This camp sparked my interest in computing, and I returned the next year as a near-peer mentor with the hope of helping girls connect their passions to STEM. I remember one girl who loved to write, so I showed her how to create interactive stories online.

This led me to join my school’s robotics club as the marketing lead, where I created digital graphics for the club and launched a new website and club logo. Being the marketing lead of my robotics club helped me connect my passion for graphic design to robotics, and my background in coding also led me to take on an active role as a programmer on my robotics team. I progressed from having no interest in coding at all to developing a passion for it and mentoring girls of all ages in STEM camps while also making significant contributions to my robotics team.

While mixing paint with pixels may seem like an odd combination or a glitch in a system, fusing art with technology is nothing short of wizardry to me. Since the start of high school, I’ve always been fascinated with how code can be used to create stunning graphics, and for the last three years, I’ve been working with creative cloud applications like Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator to explore the world of digital design. I’ve used these applications to create designs for logos, t-shirts, flyers, and animations. My creative world runs on technology, and I’m picturing a career in graphic design or even animation so I can continue to pursue my interests in art and technology. I want to dig deeper into things like web design, animation, virtual reality, and more to push the boundaries for visual communication. In a world of technology, graphic design is more important than ever, and as I continue to advance my programming and art skills, meshing code with design could be the key to helping me unlock my potential.
